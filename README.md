## What MareNostrum Is

MareNostrum is a strategic infrastructure framework. Its object is sovereign AI compute — the physical and institutional substrate that determines who controls artificial intelligence at a territorial scale.

The argument, in one paragraph:

> The Mediterranean basin receives more solar energy per square meter than any comparable inhabited region in the world. Converted to sovereign AI compute tokens at the point of production — rather than exported as low-value grid electricity through saturated interconnections — this energy is worth ×10 to ×31 more per unit. The infrastructure that performs this conversion is more robust when the populations that host it share directly in its economic output. Popular adherence is not a moral objective. It is an engineering requirement: an infrastructure that generates local income is defended; one that does not is contested, delayed, and eventually blocked.

This is the economic case for cooperative sovereign compute. It is also the strategic case for France and Europe: distributed, democratically governed AI infrastructure is harder to capture, harder to disable, and harder to monopolise than centralised hyperscaler infrastructure.

MareNostrum defines the architecture, the governance, the pricing, and the contracts for the energy + compute substrate. [FractaVolta](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/FractaVolta) operates it as an integrated four-layer stack — energy, compute, cognition ([Cogentia](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/cogentia)), and civic ([inseme](https://github.com/JeanHuguesRobert/inseme)).

## The Economic Argument

Large infrastructure projects fail for a predictable reason: the populations that host them bear the costs (visual impact, land use, noise, risk) while the revenues flow elsewhere. The result is organised opposition — legal challenges, local referendums, political blocking — that adds years and hundreds of millions of euros to project costs, or kills them entirely.

MareNostrum inverts this structure through three mechanisms:

**1. Cooperative ownership**: host communities hold equity in the infrastructure through SCIC cooperative structures with impartible reserves — a sovereign fund that cannot be sold or extracted by outside investors.

**2. Direct revenue distribution**: a portion of compute token revenues is distributed to cooperative members as a local basic income. The Plan 2038 target for Corsica: 500 €/month per cooperative member, financed entirely from local solar exergy converted to AI inference revenue.

**3. Democratic governance**: one person, one voice. Infrastructure decisions are made by those who live with their consequences, not by distant shareholders or technocrats. This is not idealism — it is risk management. Governance capture is the primary failure mode of strategic infrastructure; democratic design is the engineering countermeasure.

The result: populations become shareholders of the infrastructure they host. Opposition becomes economically irrational. Robustness is structural, not contractual.

## Strategic Context

France and Europe face a structural dependency on non-European AI compute infrastructure. The dominant providers are American hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) and, increasingly, Chinese state-backed alternatives. Both represent concentration risks — regulatory, geopolitical, and technical — that strategic planners are beginning to acknowledge but have not yet resolved.

MareNostrum proposes a third path: distributed, island-anchored, cooperatively governed sovereign compute, powered by Mediterranean solar energy and governed democratically at the territorial level.

The historical precedent is the CECA (1951) — the European Coal and Steel Community, which placed strategic industrial resources under supranational democratic governance to prevent their capture by any single national interest. MareNostrum applies the same institutional logic to AI compute and solar exergy, with the Mediterranean basin as the operational territory.

This is not a regional project that happens to be in Corsica. It is a European strategic project that begins in Corsica because the conditions — irradiance, insularity, political context, institutional history — make it the right laboratory.

### Proclamation to the Citizens of the Mediterranean Basin

*Citizens, inhabitants, local actors,*
*You who, from the Pillars of Hercules to the eastern shores, endure or benefit from the unconquerable star that lights your rooftops,*
*Listen to the voice of dawn: it comes not from lobbies, nor algorithms, nor technocrats — it comes from you.*

For today, in this blessed basin where the sun pours its gifts with unequalled generosity, **we have until now wasted abundance as a kingdom wastes its gold reserves**: we have produced without strategy, stored without vision, and forgotten that **energy is not a commodity, but the invisible foundation of our freedom**.

**The hour of citizen reconquest has come.**

---

### I. The Foundational Axiom: Energy Sovereignty = Economic Sovereignty

**Energy is not a resource like the others. It is the blood of our societies, the fuel of our economies, the safeguard of our freedoms.**

Yet today, Mediterranean peoples **suffer** their energy destiny:

- Their rooftops are leased to speculative developers.
- Their grids are controlled by distant multinationals.
- Their winter nights, where light grows scarce, are left at the mercy of faceless suppliers.

**This is unacceptable — and it is also economically irrational.**

The Mediterranean sun produces value that leaves the territory. MareNostrum returns that value to the people who live under it.

We therefore declare:

> **The Mediterranean sun belongs to the peoples who inhabit it.**
> **Its revenues shall flow to those who host the infrastructure — not to distant shareholders.**

---

### II. The Economic Promise

We do not sell kilowatt-hours. We sell a promise:
*"When the sun hides, when the winds fall silent, when demand surges, the energy will be there — and its revenue will be in your account."*

The target: **500 €/month** of basic income per cooperative member, financed entirely by local solar exergy converted to sovereign AI inference revenue. No subsidy. No dependency. 100% local production.

**An energy that generates no local income is not sovereign energy — it is rented territory.**

---

### III. The Architecture of Sovereignty: Flow, Capital, Guarantee

A sound energy system must:

1. **Flow**: produce boldly — solar panels, citizen wind, green hydrogen.
2. **Capital**: store intelligently — batteries, pumped hydro, hydrogen storage.
3. **Guarantee**: assure firmly — local microgrids, scarcity contracts, absolute traceability.

**Any system that neglects one of these three pillars is condemned to fail.**

---

### IV. Sovereign Governance: A Mandate Under Permanent Control

We reject:
- Distant energy governments.
- Faceless technocrats.
- Opaque algorithms that decide in our place.

**Our system will be:**
- **Democratic**: one person, one voice.
- **Imperative**: delegates act under permanent citizen control.
- **Revocable**: the people can recall their servants at any moment.

This is not idealism. It is risk engineering: democratic governance is the structural countermeasure against infrastructure capture.

---

### V. Traceability: The Proof That Overturns Energy Tyrants

We decree:

- **Every act of governance over common resources shall be traceable, verifiable, opposable.**
- **Every untraceable act shall be null and void.**
- **Trust shall no longer be an act of faith, but an algorithmic and human certainty.**

**An opaque energy system is a tyrannical energy system.**

---

### VI. The Mediterranean Strategy: A Sovereign Network of Light

The Mediterranean is not a lake. It is an ocean of possibility — complementary solar zones (Corsica, Sicily, Sardinia, Tunisia, Morocco, Peloponnese), diverse climates, and the potential for a network that belongs to no single nation.

**MareNostrum will not be a federation of nations — it will be a sovereign network, united by the sun and governed by the peoples.**

---

### VII. The Call to Action

We ask:
- **Produce locally.**
- **Store collectively.**
- **Guarantee sovereignly.**
- **Trace with full transparency.**
- **Decide democratically.**
- **Earn directly.**

**For energy is not a resource — it is a right. And its revenue is yours.**

---

### VIII. Conclusion

**It is an oath.**
An oath we swear today,
for our children, for our cities, for the Mediterranean.
